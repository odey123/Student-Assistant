AHMADU BELLO UNIVERSITY - FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQ)

GENERAL QUESTIONS:

Q: What is ABU?
A: Ahmadu Bello University (ABU) is a federal government research university located in Zaria, Kaduna State, Nigeria. It is one of the largest universities in Sub-Saharan Africa, founded in 1962 and named after Sir Ahmadu Bello, the Sardauna of Sokoto.

Q: Where is ABU located?
A: ABU is located in Zaria, Kaduna State, Nigeria. The university has two main campuses: Samaru (main campus) and Kongo. The teaching hospital (ABUTH) is at Shika.

Q: What is ABU's motto?
A: "Discipline, Self-Reliance and Excellence" (Unofficially often associated with "Naturally Ahead")

Q: Is ABU a federal or state university?
A: ABU is a federal university owned and operated by the Federal Government of Nigeria.

ADMISSION QUESTIONS:

Q: What is the JAMB cut-off for ABU?
A: The minimum JAMB score for Post-UTME eligibility is usually 180. However, departmental admission cut-offs are higher and vary significantly. Competitive courses like Medicine and Veterinary Medicine often require 200+ JAMB scores.

Q: Does ABU accept two sittings for O'Level?
A: Yes, ABU accepts relevant O'Level credits obtained in not more than two (2) sittings from recognized examination bodies (WAEC, NECO, NABTEB, GCE).

Q: Is Post-UTME compulsory for ABU?
A: Yes, all candidates must participate in the ABU Post-UTME screening exercise. The format (CBT or credential screening) should be confirmed yearly.

Q: How do I apply for ABU admission?
A: First, register for JAMB and choose ABU as your first choice. After UTME, wait for ABU to announce Post-UTME registration, then apply through the ABU portal.

Q: What are the O'Level requirements?
A: Minimum of 5 O'Level credit passes in relevant subjects, including English Language and Mathematics, obtained in not more than two sittings. Specific subject requirements vary by course.

FEES AND PAYMENT QUESTIONS:

Q: How much are ABU school fees?
A: School fees vary by faculty, level, and residency status. Fresh students (non-indigenes) typically pay ₦90,000 - ₦150,000+ per session. Indigenes of Kaduna State pay slightly lower. Medicine, Veterinary Medicine, and Engineering are typically higher. Returning students pay ₦40,000 - ₦80,000+.

Q: Are ABU fees the same for indigenes and non-indigenes?
A: No, indigenes of Kaduna State pay slightly lower fees than non-indigenes. Proof of indigeneship (Certificate of Origin) is required.

Q: How do I pay school fees?
A: Log into the ABU Student Portal (https://portal.abu.edu.ng/), generate a Transaction ID/Invoice, and pay using Remita (online or at designated banks).

Q: Can I pay fees in installments?
A: Check with the Bursar's Office for current installment payment policies. Payment schedules are set by the university.

CAMPUS LIFE QUESTIONS:

Q: Does ABU have multiple campuses?
A: Yes, ABU has two main campuses: Samaru (main) and Kongo. The teaching hospital (ABUTH) is at Shika.

Q: Does ABU provide hostel accommodation?
A: Yes, ABU has extensive hostel facilities on both Samaru and Kongo campuses. Famous halls include Suleiman Hall, Danfodio Hall, Icsa Hall, Ribadu Hall, Amina Hall, and Queen Elizabeth Hall. Allocation is competitive and managed by Student Affairs Division.

Q: Is there transportation between campuses?
A: Yes, inter-campus transportation services are available between Samaru and Kongo campuses.

Q: Is there a library at ABU?
A: Yes, the main library is the Kashim Ibrahim Library (KIL) on the Samaru campus, one of the largest university libraries in Nigeria. There's also the President Kennedy Library on Kongo campus and a Medical Library at Shika.

Q: Is there a teaching hospital?
A: Yes, Ahmadu Bello University Teaching Hospital (ABUTH) is located at Shika, Zaria. It's one of the major teaching hospitals in Northern Nigeria.

ACADEMIC QUESTIONS:

Q: What is the ABU student portal?
A: The ABU Student Portal (https://portal.abu.edu.ng/) is used for most academic processes including course registration, fee payment, and result checking.

Q: How do I register for courses?
A: After paying school fees, log into the ABU portal, navigate to Course Registration, select your courses based on your curriculum, submit, and print your course form.

Q: How do I check my results?
A: Log into your account on the ABU Student Portal. Results are typically released online 4-8 weeks after examinations.

Q: What faculties does ABU have?
A: ABU has 15 faculties including Administration, Agriculture, Arts, Education, Engineering, Environmental Design, Law, Life Sciences, Physical Sciences, Social Sciences, Veterinary Medicine, Pharmaceutical Sciences, Basic Clinical Sciences, Clinical Sciences, and Allied Health Sciences.

Q: Does ABU use CBT for exams?
A: ABU uses primarily written examinations. Some large courses or general studies may utilize CBT.

PRACTICAL QUESTIONS:

Q: How do I get to ABU from other parts of Nigeria?
A: Zaria is accessible by road, rail, and air (nearest airport is Kaduna). The university is located in Samaru and Kongo areas of Zaria. Local transportation (taxis, buses, keke) is available.

Q: What should I bring for resumption?
A: Essential items include admission documents, personal clothing, toiletries, bedding (check hostel requirements), study materials, laptop (recommended), and adequate funds for personal expenses.

Q: Are there banking facilities on campus?
A: Yes, there are banking facilities and ATM machines on campus for student convenience.

Q: Do I need to attend classes on different campuses?
A: It depends on your program. Most programs are on Samaru campus. Some faculties/departments are on Kongo campus. Transportation is available between campuses.

SPECIAL FEATURES:

Q: What makes ABU unique?
A: ABU is distinguished by:
- One of the largest universities in Sub-Saharan Africa
- Oldest federal university in Northern Nigeria
- Extensive research facilities (IAR, NAPRI, CERT)
- Strong veterinary medicine and agriculture programs
- Kashim Ibrahim Library (one of Nigeria's largest)
- Rich history and tradition
- Comprehensive academic programs

Q: Are there research opportunities?
A: Yes, ABU has several research institutes including the Institute for Agricultural Research (IAR), National Animal Production Research Institute (NAPRI), and Centre for Energy Research and Training (CERT). Students can participate in research projects.

Q: Does ABU offer postgraduate programs?
A: Yes, ABU has a School of Postgraduate Studies offering Master's and PhD programs across various disciplines.

Q: What is SBRS?
A: School of Basic and Remedial Studies (SBRS) is for students who need foundational courses before proceeding to their main programs.
