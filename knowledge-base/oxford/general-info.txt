Overview: The University of Oxford, located in Oxford, England, is the oldest university in the English-speaking world and is regarded as one of the world's leading academic institutions. It is a collegiate research university, meaning the university is composed of numerous self-governing colleges, alongside central academic departments and administrative bodies.

History:

c. 1096: Evidence of teaching dates back to 1096, making it the oldest university in the English-speaking world.

1167: Growth accelerated significantly when Henry II banned English students from attending the University of Paris.

1231: Received a Royal Charter.

The collegiate system gradually emerged from the 13th century onwards, with colleges like University College, Balliol, and Merton being among the earliest.

Oxford played significant roles throughout British history, including during the Reformation and the English Civil War. It continued to expand and modernize, admitting women in the late 19th century and establishing numerous research departments in the 20th and 21st centuries.
